UX Design I Google Certificate Program I 2021

Project Overview

Project Vision

Grocery Store is a conceptual grocery shop located in the suburbs of a metropolitan area. Grocery Store strives to deliver fresh grocery items, and many other daily necessities of people. It targets customers like students and workers who lack the time to do grocery shopping and helps people by delivering products on their doorsteps so that they don’t have to get outside during this pandemic.

Problem

Busy workers and commuters lack the time necessary to shop grocery items.

Goal

Design an app for Grocery Store that allows users to order fresh, healthy grocery items and other necessary daily items easily.

My Role

UX designer. Designing an app for Grocery Store from conception to delivery.

Responsibilities

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

Duration

September 2021 to October 2021

User Research

Understanding the User

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I was designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was working adults who don’t have time to grocery shopping.
This user group confirmed initial assumptions about Grocery Store customers, but research also revealed that time was not the only factor limiting users from going to grocery shops. Other user problems included obligations, interests, or challenges (like the covid pandemic issue) that make it challenging to get groceries.

User Pain Points

1. Time

Working adults are too busy to spend time on grocery shopping

2. Accessibility

Platforms for ordering groceries are not equipped with assistive technologies

3.IA

Text-heavy menus in apps are often difficult to read and order from

4. Safety

Easier to order online to ensure health safety during this pandemic

Information Architecture

The main goal of this app is to create a usable and enjoyable experience for the user. I designed the app's content in such a way that the users can easily navigate through the app and find whatever they want to order easily while keeping track of their product.

Test and Iteration

Usability Study Findings

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 Findings

  • 1. Users want to save their card information
  • 2. Users want large icons
  • 3. Users want to track their order

Round 2 Findings

  • 1. Users want to see the number of items into the cart
  • 2. Users want to be notified after adding something into the cart

Mockups

Early designs allowed for some customization, but after the usability studies, I added additional options to track the order. I also revised the design so users can see the number of items present at the cart from anywhere on the app.

The second usability study revealed frustration with product adding to the cart. Users want to be notified while adding items to the cart. So, I added a screen to notify users after adding items to the cart. I also added an option to see the number of items present on the cart.

Accessibility Consideration

1. Multiple languages

Provided multiple languages to users who are not fluent in English.

2. Icons

Used icons to help make navigation easier.

3.Images

Used detailed imagery for pizzas and toppings to help all users better understand the designs.

4. Screen readers

Provided access to users who are vision impaired through adding alt text to images for screen readers.

4. Colors

Used such colors that everyone can easily see & differentiate between the foreground & background.

Design System

Visual Design

When designing this app's visual style, I tried to use colors that helped me convey the feeling of comfort to the users. I used the shades of green, keeping accessibility in mind. I selected a sans-serif typeface that blends well with the overall visual design of the app. Therefore, I chose Monsterrat as the primary typeface of the app.

Going Forward

Takeaways

Impact

The app makes users feel like Grocery Store really thinks about how to meet their needs.
One quote from peer feedback:
“The app is so easy to navigate and order from that I would definitely use this app to order my groceries and track the order while staying at home during this pandemic.”

What I Learned

While designing the Grocery Store app, I learned that the first ideas for the app are only the beginning of the process. Usability studies and peer feedback influenced each iteration of the app’s designs.

Future Steps

I am proud of what I have designed. However, if I get a chance, I would love to iterate on my design according to the following steps-.

Testing

Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed.

User Research

Conduct more user research to determine any new areas of need.
